**Core offer (productized service): “Clone Store Takedown Sprint (24–72h)”**
- **Goal:** reduce ongoing losses fast by removing/neutralizing clones and blocking traffic sources where possible.

## 4) Risk map + mitigation

### Risk 1 — “You can’t guarantee takedowns” (platform/registrar inertia)
- **Mitigation:** Sell *process + speed + evidence quality + follow-ups*, not guaranteed removal. Contract language: “best-effort.”

### Risk 2 — Legal/unauthorized practice concerns
- **Mitigation:** Position as **technical incident operations**. No legal advice. Provide “when to talk to a lawyer” guidance + optional referral partner.

### Risk 3 — Scammers re-appear quickly (whack-a-mole)
- **Mitigation:** Retainer includes monitoring + rapid re-filing + domain-variant purchasing recommendations.

### Risk 4 — Prospect skepticism (“I can just report it myself”)
- **Mitigation:** Emphasize:
  - evidence pack quality + completeness
  - time cost + follow-up discipline
  - speed: “first filings in 2 hours”
  - structured tracker + escalation templates

### Risk 5 — Distribution stalls (no steady inbound)
- **Mitigation:** Lead with agency partnerships (multiplier), then add outbound via Ad Library. Build 1-page landing + proof artifacts from first 1–2 sprints.

### Risk 6 — Reputation risk (accusing wrong sites)
- **Mitigation:** Add a verification step: only file claims when there’s clear brand impersonation (copied creatives, confusingly similar domain, fake checkout, etc.). Keep an internal “evidence threshold” checklist.
